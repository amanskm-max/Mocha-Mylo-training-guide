0. Training Philosophy — Read This First

You are not "training a dog". You are building a relationship with a tiny intelligent animal who wants two things: to feel safe, and to make you happy. Every interaction teaches him something — whether you meant to or not.

Toy poodles are ranked the #2 smartest breed in the world. That cuts both ways: he will learn "sit" in 5 reps, but he will also learn "if I bark Dad gives me attention" in 2 reps. So be intentional.

The one-line rule: Reward what you want. Ignore or prevent what you don't. Never punish.

Modern dog training is built on one science: positive reinforcement. Behaviors that get rewarded get repeated. That's it. The old "alpha / dominance / pack leader" stuff has been debunked for 20+ years and it shuts down sensitive breeds like poodles for life. Forget it forever.

1. Your Training Toolkit

Treats — your currency

Treats are paychecks. Tiny, soft, smelly, swallowed in <1 second (no chewing — kills the training rhythm).

  • Tier 1 (everyday): his regular kibble — use for easy stuff at home
  • Tier 2 (medium): JerHigh sticks broken into pea-sized bits, Pedigree puppy treats
  • Tier 3 (jackpot): boiled chicken, boiled paneer, tiny bits of cheese — for hard stuff (recall, vet, distractions)

Size: pea-sized. He weighs 1 kg. Anything bigger fills him up in 5 reps.

Daily treat budget: max 10% of his food calories. Subtract treats from his meal portions so he doesn't get fat.

Gear

  • Treat pouch (clip-on, opens fast) — game changer
  • Soft H-harness (NEVER attach leash to a neck collar)
  • 1.2 m flat leash — no retractables
  • Clicker (optional) or just your voice marker "Yes!"
  • Lick mat (for grooming & bath training)
  • Snuffle mat / Kong Puppy / lick toys (mental tiring)
  • Pee pads + enzymatic cleaner (Drools / Nature's Miracle)
  • Bait bag of his kibble — leave one in every room

2. The 10 Universal Training Rules

  1. Short, often, fun. 2–5 minute sessions, 3–5 times a day. Stop while he still wants more.
  2. One word, one meaning. Pick "sit" or "baith" — never both. Same word, same tone, every time.
  3. Reward within 1 second. After 2 seconds he doesn't connect treat to behavior.
  4. Lure → Reward → Cue. First lure with a treat, then add the word, then fade the lure.
  5. End on a win. Always finish with something he nails, then a jackpot treat.
  6. Train hungry, not starving. Train before meals, not after.
  7. Train calm, not tired. Overtired puppies = nipping & tantrums. Stop and nap.
  8. Generalise. A "sit" learned in your kitchen is NOT a "sit" in the lift lobby. Practice everywhere.
  9. Never punish a recall. If he comes when called, it's always good. Never call him to do something he hates.
  10. Be boring when he's wrong, exciting when he's right. No drama for mistakes. Big party for wins.

3. The Marker System ("Yes!")

A marker is a sound that means "that exact thing you just did earned you a treat". It's how you tell him precisely which behaviour you liked, even before the treat reaches his mouth.

Choose ONE marker word. I recommend a crisp, happy "YES!" Use it for every reward, forever.

Charging the marker (Day 1 — 5 minutes)

1
Sit on the floor with 20 pea-sized treats.
2
Say "Yes!" → immediately give a treat. He doesn't have to do anything.
3
Wait 3–5 sec. Repeat. 20 times.
4
Test: when he's looking away, say "Yes!" If his head whips around expecting food — it's charged. Done.

From now on, "Yes!" = a treat is coming. Never say "Yes!" without delivering the treat, or you'll devalue it.

4. A Day in the Life — Sample Schedule

Toy puppies need a predictable rhythm. Routine = security = calm dog. Adapt these times to your day.

06:30 Wake up → potty (carry to pad) → "Yes!" + treat 06:45 Breakfast (in his bowl OR hand-fed for bonding/training) 07:00 Potty 07:05 Training session #1 (3 min) — name, sit, look 07:10 Free play / chew toy 07:30 NAP (1.5–2 hr) 09:30 Wake → potty → small training (1 min recall game) 10:00 Calm time / lick mat / snuffle mat 10:30 NAP 12:30 Potty → lunch → potty 12:45 Training session #2 (4 min) — sit, down, name 13:00 Socialisation (carry him out of the house, let him see the world) 13:30 NAP 15:30 Potty → snack/training treats → handling practice (paws, ears, mouth) 16:00 NAP 17:30 Potty → free play with you on the floor 18:00 Training session #3 (4 min) — review + 1 new thing 18:15 Calm chew time 19:00 Dinner → potty 19:30 Family cuddle / TV time (in pen, on his bed) 20:30 Last training (2 min easy wins, end happy) 21:00 Last potty 21:15 Wind-down — dim lights, soft voice 21:30 Crate / sleep 00:30 Potty break (week 1–3 only) 03:30 Potty break (week 1–2 only)
By week 4–6, drop the night potty breaks one at a time. By month 4, he should sleep through the night.

6. Lesson 1 — Name Recognition

Week 1 The single most important thing you'll teach him. His name = "look at me, good things happen".

How

1
Sit 1 m from him with 20 treats.
2
Say "Mocha!" in a happy voice — ONCE.
3
The instant his eyes flick to you → "Yes!" → treat.
4
If he doesn't look in 2 seconds, make a small kissy noise to get his attention, then "Yes!" → treat. Don't repeat his name.
5
Repeat 10 times. Stop. Do another set 1 hour later.

Watch out

  • NEVER use his name when angry. "MOCHA NO!" poisons the name forever.
  • NEVER repeat the name 5 times. He learns to ignore it. Say it ONCE.
  • Don't use his name + "come" in week 1. Just the name.

7. Lesson 2 — Sit

Week 2 The first "trick". 3 minutes a day, learned in 2–4 days.

How — the lure method

1
Hold a treat between your thumb and finger, right at his nose.
2
Slowly move the treat up and slightly back over his head.
3
His nose follows up → his bum drops down. The instant his bum touches the floor → "Yes!" → treat right at his nose (not above, or he'll stand again).
4
Repeat 5 times. If he gets it, do 5 more.
5
Day 2: same lure, but now say "Sit" right as his bum starts dropping.
6
Day 3: lure with an empty hand (same motion). Say "Sit". Bum drops → "Yes!" → treat from your other hand.
7
Day 4: just say "Sit" with a tiny hand cue. Reward. The lure is faded.

Common bugs

  • Treat too high → he jumps. Lower it.
  • Treat too far back → he walks backward. Closer to nose.
  • You said "sit" three times → he learned "sit-sit-sit" means sit. Always one word.

8. Lesson 3 — "Look at Me" / Watch

Week 2 The cure for distraction. Whenever he's losing focus, "look" brings him back.

How

1
Hold a treat at his nose. Slowly raise it to your chin/eye.
2
His eyes follow → meets yours → "Yes!" → treat.
3
After 5 reps, add the cue "Look" or "Watch me" right before raising the treat.
4
Build duration: 1 sec eye contact → 2 sec → 3 sec before marking.
5
Practice in noisy rooms, then outside. This is your emergency focus button.

9. Lesson 4 — Recall (Come)

Week 3 The most important command of his life. A reliable recall can literally save him from a car or a snake.

The Recall Game (indoors only at first)

1
Two people, opposite ends of a small room. 10 treats each.
2
Person A squats, opens arms, in a happy excited voice: "Mocha, come!"
3
The instant he turns and starts moving → cheer "yes yes yes!" → he runs to you → 3 treats in a row + cuddle party.
4
Person B repeats from their end. Back and forth, 5 rounds.
5
Stop while he's still excited.

Solo recall

Walk into another room → call him → "Yes!" + jackpot when he arrives. Random surprise recalls during the day = he learns coming to dad is always magic.

Sacred recall rules

  • NEVER call him for something bad — bath, nail trim, crate at end of fun. Go get him instead.
  • NEVER repeat "come". If he ignores once, walk over, gently pick him up, and reset.
  • Use a SPECIAL recall treat (boiled chicken) that only happens for recall. Nothing else.
  • Outdoor recall: only on a long line (5 m) until rock solid. The world is too tempting.

10. Lesson 5 — Down

Week 4

How

1
Start with him in a "Sit".
2
Treat at nose → slowly lower it straight down to the floor between his front paws.
3
As his head follows down, slowly drag the treat forward along the floor away from him.
4
His elbows drop → belly touches → "Yes!" → treat on the floor.
5
After 5 reps, add the cue "Down" as he lowers.
6
Fade the lure over 3–4 days into a flat-palm-down hand signal.

Tip: If he just bends his head, try it next to a low coffee table — he has to crawl under to follow the treat. Forces a down.

11. Lesson 6 — Stay

Week 5 Build in 3 dimensions: Duration, Distance, Distraction — but only ONE at a time.

How

1
Sit him. Say "Stay" + show flat palm out (like a stop sign).
2
Wait 1 second. "Yes!" → treat → release word "OK!" or "free!"
3
Build duration: 1s → 2s → 3s → 5s → 10s. Add a second only after 3 successes.
4
Once 10s is solid, reset to 1s but take a half step back. Build distance.
5
Once distance is solid, reset to 1s short distance and add a small distraction (drop a sock, clap softly).

Critical rule

Always go back TO him to reward — don't call him out of stay. The release word is the only thing that ends the stay. Otherwise he learns "stay = wait until you get bored and walk away".

12. Lesson 7 — Leave It

Week 5 The "don't eat that dead pigeon" command. Possibly life-saving in Mumbai streets.

How — the closed fist game

1
Put a treat in your closed fist. Show it to him. He'll lick, paw, nibble.
2
Say nothing. Wait. The instant he pulls his nose away — even 1 cm — "Yes!" → treat from your OTHER hand (never the fist treat).
3
Repeat 10 times. He'll start backing off faster.
4
Now add the cue "Leave it" as you present the fist.
5
Level up: open palm with treat. If he goes for it, close the fist. Wait for the back-off. "Yes!" → treat from other hand.
6
Level up: treat on the floor under your shoe. Same game.
7
Level up: treat on the floor uncovered. You stand ready to cover it. Same game.
8
Final test: drop something on the walk → "Leave it!" → he looks at you → jackpot.

13. Lesson 8 — Drop It

Week 4

Different from "leave it". Drop = "spit out what's already in your mouth".

1
Give him a low-value toy he likes.
2
Hold a high-value treat near his nose. He'll drop the toy to get the food.
3
As the toy drops → "Yes!" → treat → return the toy.
4
After 5 reps, add cue "Drop" right before the trade.
Always trade for something better. If you snatch things away, he learns to guard and run. Trading builds trust.

14. Lesson 9 — Wait at Doors & Bowls

Week 6 Door darting kills small dogs. This is the prevention.

Door wait

1
Stand at the door, hand on the knob. Say "Wait".
2
Open the door 2 cm. If he moves forward → close it gently. No drama.
3
If he holds → "Yes!" → treat → release "OK!" → walk through together.
4
Build to fully open door and you stepping through first.

Bowl wait

Hold his food bowl. Say "Wait". Lower it slowly. If he lunges, lift it back up. When he hesitates → "Yes!" → place bowl → "OK eat!". Within a week he'll auto-sit when he sees the bowl.

15. Lesson 10 — Place / Settle on a Mat

Week 6 Calm-on-cue. Worth its weight in gold when you have guests, eat dinner, or work from home.

1
Put a small mat or bed on the floor.
2
Toss a treat on it. He goes to investigate. The instant his paws are on the mat → "Yes!" → another treat ON the mat.
3
Wait. He'll wander off. Toss another treat on the mat. Repeat.
4
After 10 reps, he'll volunteer onto the mat. NOW add the cue "Place" or "Bed".
5
Build duration: when he lies down on his own → big jackpot. Slowly extend to 30s, 1 min, 5 min by tossing treats every X seconds.
6
Use during your dinner. He learns: dinner time = mat time = treats happen.

16. Lesson 11 — Loose Leash & Heel

Week 7+ Only after final vaccinations. Until then, practice indoors with the leash & harness on (no walking, just wearing).

The "be a tree" method

1
Put harness + leash on. Stand still. The instant the leash is loose (any slack) → "Yes!" → treat at your left thigh.
2
Take ONE step. Loose leash → "Yes!" → treat at thigh.
3
If he pulls → STOP dead. Do not move forward. Wait until the leash goes loose (he turns, looks, steps back). The instant it's loose → "Yes!" → continue.
4
Pulling = walk stops. Loose = walk continues. He works it out in a week.

Heel position

Heel = walking right at your left side, watching you. Lure him into position with a treat at your thigh. Take 2 steps → "Yes!" → treat. Build to 5, 10, 20 steps.

17. Lesson 12 — Quiet (No Bark)

Week 6+ Poodles bark. Lots. Don't try to make him never bark — teach him to STOP on cue.

1
Wait for him to bark (doorbell, etc.). Let him bark 2–3 times.
2
Hold a treat at his nose. He has to stop barking to sniff it.
3
The instant he's quiet for 1 second → say "Quiet" → "Yes!" → treat.
4
Build to 3s, 5s, 10s of silence.
NEVER yell "QUIET!" at a barking dog. To him you're barking back — joining in. Stay calm.

18. Crate Training

Week 1 onwards Crate = his bedroom, his safe space, never punishment.

1
Day 1: door open, blanket inside, toss treats in. Let him walk in & out freely. Don't close the door.
2
Day 2: feed his meals just inside the crate, door open.
3
Day 3: feed meals fully inside. While he eats, close door for 5 seconds → open before he finishes.
4
Day 4: closed for 30 sec while you sit beside him, talking softly.
5
Day 5: closed for 2 min. Walk a few steps away. Come back, open.
6
Day 6: 5 min, you leave the room.
7
Day 7+: 10 min, 20 min, with a frozen Kong of plain yogurt to chew.

Cue: "Crate" or "Bed"

Toss treat in → say "Crate" as he walks in → "Yes!" → second treat inside. After a week he'll go in on cue.

Crating rules

  • Never let him out while he's whining — wait for 3 sec of quiet, then open. Otherwise he learns whine = freedom.
  • Tired puppy goes in crate. Wired puppy goes for play first.
  • Cover 3 sides with cloth = den feeling.
  • Max crate time: age in months + 1 = hours (3 months = 4 hrs max). Never more than 5 hrs ever.

19. Potty Training (the parallel mission)

This runs alongside everything else. Toy breeds take 4–6 months to be reliably house-trained — they have walnut-sized bladders.

The system

1
Confine him to a small area (pen) when you can't watch. Bigger space = more accidents.
2
Carry him to the pee pad: after every nap, after every meal, after every play burst, every 45–60 min otherwise.
3
Stand still. Boring. Don't talk. Wait up to 3 min.
4
The instant he starts going, softly say a cue word — "Su-su" or "Potty". Same word every time.
5
When he finishes → "YES! Good potty!" → treat → 30 sec play as bonus reward.
6
If he has an accident off-pad: silently clean with enzymatic cleaner. NO scolding. Make a mental note: "I missed his cue".

Reading his cues

Sniffing the floor in circles = about to go. Whining at the pen door = about to go. Sudden stop in play = about to go. Carry him to the pad now.

Pad → outdoors

After full vaccination (~16 wk), slowly move the pad closer to the door, then outside the door, then carry him to the same spot in the garden. Use the same cue word. Same treat. Within 2–3 weeks he'll prefer outside.

20. Bite Inhibition

Puppy teeth feel like needles. Goal isn't "no biting ever" — it's "soft mouth, then no teeth on humans at all".

1
When he bites you, give a sharp "OUCH!" in a high pitch (like a hurt puppy). Freeze. Be boring for 10 seconds.
2
Redirect to a chew toy. Praise him for chewing the toy.
3
If he keeps biting, stand up and walk out of the pen for 30 seconds. Come back calm.
4
Repeat. He learns: teeth on skin = fun ends.

Never

  • Smack his nose
  • Hold his mouth shut
  • Wrestle / rough play with hands (teaches biting hands)
  • Yank your hand back fast (looks like prey)

Most puppies stop hard biting by 5 months. Be patient.

21. Handling & Grooming Cooperation

Poodles get groomed for life. Train him to LOVE being touched everywhere. 1 minute a day.

1
Touch his paw for 1 second → "Yes!" → treat. Repeat with each paw.
2
Touch his ear flap → "Yes!" → treat. Look inside → "Yes!" → treat.
3
Lift his lip, touch a tooth → "Yes!" → treat. Build to a finger toothbrush.
4
Run brush through his coat one stroke → "Yes!" → treat.
5
Touch nail clipper to a nail (don't clip yet) → "Yes!" → treat. Build to one tiny clip a day.
6
Lick mat with peanut butter (xylitol-free!) stuck to bathroom wall = best bath training tool ever.

22. Alone-Time Training (preventing separation anxiety)

Toy poodles are velcro dogs. Without training, they can't be left alone without panicking. Start day 2.

1
Step out of the room for 10 seconds. Come back. No fuss.
2
Build to 30 sec, 1 min, 2 min, 5 min. Always come back BEFORE he panics.
3
Add a "I'll be back" ritual: a specific phrase + a frozen Kong as you leave.
4
When you return: ignore him for 30 sec. No big greeting (it teaches him your return is a huge dramatic thing = stress).
5
Build to 10 min, 30 min, 1 hr, 2 hr alone. Use a camera to monitor.
Goals by age: 8–10 wk = 30 min alone. 3 mo = 2 hr. 4 mo = 3 hr. 6 mo = 4 hr. Adult = 5 hr max.

23. Fun Tricks (after the basics)

Paw / Shake 1. Sit. Hold a treat in your closed fist near the floor.
2. He paws at your fist to get it → "Yes!" → treat.
3. Add cue "Paw" → fade fist → just open palm.
Spin 1. Treat at nose → slowly circle around his body.
2. He follows in a full circle → "Yes!" → treat.
3. Add cue "Spin". Fade lure to a finger circle.
High Five After "Paw" is solid: hold your hand higher and vertical. He raises paw to it → "Yes!" → treat. New cue "Five".
Roll Over From "Down": lure with treat from nose → over shoulder → he rolls. Reward generously, this is hard.
Bow Lure his nose down between his front paws while keeping his bum up. Capture the moment → "Yes!".
Touch (nose to hand) Present open palm. Curiosity makes him bump it with nose → "Yes!" → treat. Add cue "Touch". This is also a great recall substitute.

25. Common First-Time Dad Mistakes

  • ❌ Repeating cues 5 times — teaches him to ignore the first 4.
  • ❌ Long sessions — toy puppies have 2–5 minute attention spans.
  • ❌ Treats too big — fills him up in 3 reps.
  • ❌ Training right after meals — he's full and sleepy.
  • ❌ Calling him "come" then giving a bath — recall poisoned.
  • ❌ Punishing accidents — he learns to hide pee, not stop.
  • ❌ Letting kids drag him around — fear imprint.
  • ❌ Inconsistent words ("sit" today, "baith" tomorrow).
  • ❌ Asking too much too soon (10 sec stay on day 1).
  • ❌ Skipping socialisation because "he's not vaccinated yet" — carry him out, the world must come to him.
  • ❌ Comparing to Instagram puppies. Yours is normal.
  • ❌ Quitting when frustrated. Set him up to succeed and try again tomorrow.

The 30-day promise

Do 3 sessions × 3 minutes a day for 30 days. By day 30 Mocha will know: name, sit, down, come (indoors), look, leave it, drop it, his crate cue, place, and the start of stay. That's a better-trained dog than 80% of adults you'll meet on a Mumbai walk.

🐩 Final Word

You don't need to be a dog whisperer. You need to be consistent, kind, and quick with treats. Mocha will teach you as much as you teach him. Train little, train often, end on a win, breathe through the chaos. In a year you'll have a dog that follows you around like a shadow because he chose to — not because you forced him to.
